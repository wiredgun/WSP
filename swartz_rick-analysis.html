Site Intent

The purpose of redesigning glucalite.com is to structure the site to better reach the broad spectrum of English-speaking people around the world that suffer with blood sugar issues. It is believed by eliminating the superfluous sales-oriented content, and replacing it with educationally oriented content will lead to in enhanced credibility and as a consequence improved marketability.

By focusing on a high-frequency rotation of more meaningful content will also allow the site to benefit from Google Caffeine, and other algorithmic changes to their search engine.

Competitive Analysis

Competition in this market is steeped with inferior players, and therefore we have recommended that GlucALite® reposition itself as the leader in quality, value, and service rather than another low-priced player in the market.

Customer Demographic

EDUCATION: Not necessarily college educated, although a measure of academic curiosity is indicative of persons who would be disposed to seek answers to their health concerns.

COMPUTER SKILLS: An ability to conduct research online, along with a moderate understanding of the Internet would be typical of a person that would be interested in GlucALite or similar products.

AGE GROUP: 35 years of age and older, mostly male, but a growing number of females are being diagnosed with the condition.

INCOME: Though money will not necessarily represent a genuine interest in GlucALite, or lack thereof, it does represent a likely barrier to entry for many who are “economically challenged”.

CURRENT SUPPLEMENT USER: Anyone that has taken multi-vitamins in the past is capable of appreciating the value of natural supplements.

ETHNIC MAKEUP: Blood sugar issues are not bound by race or ethnic background with the growing popularity of the unhealthy western diet. Although historically speaking, Caucasian males and people of African decent have demonstrated a susceptibility to the condition. However, peoples of all races and ethnic backgrounds who consume western fast foods and processed foodstuffs have become susceptible to the condition that leads to diabetes.

GEOGRAPHY: Like ethnicity, the geographic makeup of the population battling blood sugar issues closely follows the expansion history of western-style cuisine. In particular, fast food restaurants that specialize in menus high in gluten and other sources of carbohydrates. Prior to McDonald’s entry in to China, levels of elevated blood sugar, and morbid obesity were unheard of. Today, elevated blood sugar and diabetes have become a major health concerns for the Chinese in direct correlation with the proliferation of dining establishments such as McDonald’s, KFC, Pizza Hut, and Wendy’s to name a few. As a result of this corporate expansion and the export of western lifestyles, diabetes has become a global epidemic.

LANGUAGE: Fluent English-speakers, or possess a moderately well developed level of English.

Targeted Analysis

The target audience for glucalite.com comprises both commercial and end-users. To pinpoint one particular end-user would be very difficult as the one common thread they share is an unhealthy diet; it spans every economic class, race, religion, and sex. Blood sugar concerns can and do affect everybody, and there can be no doubt you, or someone you know, is either directly or indirectly affected by the condition. Today, it is estimated that high blood sugar affects more than 700 million people worldwide.

End-Users: These people are English-speaking, can comprise either male or female, from any ethnic background, normally over the age of 35 years (pre-diabetic and type 2 diabetic). They live in or near a major city of the world, and tend to be competent Internet users who have previously purchased products from the Internet. They have completed high school, and possess an above average intellect.

Commercial Users: The commercial customer will be a professional working in the field of natural health, and/or homeopathy. They will service a clientele that possess the characteristics that closely follow those of our end-users, possibly without the Internet skills. Their practice may be in a urban, suburban, or rural area in nearly any country in the world.
